All About Beagles

Beagles are a small to medium-sized breed of dog known for their keen sense of smell and tracking instincts. The Beagle is a breed of small scent hound, similar in appearance to the much larger foxhound. The beagle was developed primarily for hunting hare, known as beagling. Possessing a great sense of smell and superior tracking instincts, the beagle is the primary breed used as a detection dog for prohibited agricultural imports and foodstuffs in quarantine around the world. The beagle is intelligent and is a popular pet due to its size, good temper, and a lack of inherited health problems.

Beagle Trivia

  1. Beagles have one of the best-developed senses of smell among dog breeds.
  2. They were originally bred for hunting hares and rabbits.
  3. Beagles are very friendly and make excellent family pets.
  4. Snoopy from the Peanuts comic strip is a Beagle.
  5. They are known to be vocal and have a unique baying howl.
  6. Height - 13–16 in (33–41 cm)
  7. Weight - 22–25 lb (10.0–11.3 kg)
  8. Coat -Short haired, hard coat of medium length
  9. Colour -Tricolor or white in combination with black & tan/brown or brown/tan
  10. Life span - 12–15 years
